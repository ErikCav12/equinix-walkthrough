How we calculate the ROI

The math behind every dollar figure on the Financial Leader-facing ROI report — audit-level transparent, no black-box multipliers.

1The formula at a glance

Every ROI number in the report is a sum of three quantifiable efficiency components computed per use case, scaled across three scenarios, and compared against the selected deployment tier's annual cost.

base_total_per_use_case = fte_recapture + error_reduction + outsourced_avoidance aggregate_base = sum(base_total) across all use cases tier_roi_percent = ((aggregate_base − tier_annual_cost) / tier_annual_cost) × 100

Design principle: the headline number on the report always traces back to specific banded answers the Financial Leader gave. Nothing is estimated, extrapolated, or LLM-generated. The LLM writes prose; it doesn't touch dollars.

2The three benefit components

Each component captures one distinct dollar mechanism. We sum them rather than multiply because they represent independent cash flows.

FTE recapture usually the biggest

Salary + overhead released when AI automates a process. Only a fraction counts as hard savings — the other half is assumed to be redeployed to higher-value work.

fte_recapture = fte_midpoint × cost_per_fte_midpoint × industry_automation_rate × data_readiness_modifier × 0.5

The trailing × 0.5 is the "recapture share" — a deliberately conservative choice so Finance Leaders don't get a magic-bullet number that breaks under scrutiny.

Error / rework reduction

40% of annual error-cost avoided once AI takes over the workflow. Based on industry benchmarks for automation-led quality gains.

error_reduction = error_cost_midpoint × 0.40

Outsourced-spend avoidance

50% of annual contractor / outsourcing spend displaced by AI. Treats the "N/A" answer as $0 to avoid inflating numbers for respondents who don't outsource at all.

outsourced_avoidance = outsourced_spend_midpoint × 0.50

3How banded answers become numbers

Finance Leaders pick bands (e.g. "16–30 FTEs", "<$100K"), not exact figures. Each band has a deterministic midpoint. These are fixed constants in the scoring module — not LLM guesses.

Trade-off: banded midpoints compress real variance. A "<$100K" answer becomes $50K whether the actual number is $10K or $90K. Intentional — the speed-to-answer gain outweighs the precision loss, and the three-scenario range (conservative / base / optimistic) fans out the uncertainty. Respondents who want tighter numbers can add a free-text note per use case.

6Turning dollars into a ROI %

The headline ROI % compares the year-one aggregate benefit against the annual cost of the respondent's chosen deployment tier.

tier_roi_percent = ((aggregate_base − tier_annual_cost) / tier_annual_cost) × 100 Displayed rounded to the nearest 10%.

Tier annual costs:

TierAnnual costWhen to recommend
Multi-tenant (Shared Starter Pack)$48,000Low-to-moderate sensitivity, speed-to-value priority
Hybrid$120,000Mixed workloads, some need full isolation
Single-tenant (Full Starter Pack)$360,000Regulation / sovereignty / IP-sensitive

Why round to 10%? The "~" prefix on the hero signals "approximate" — this is a directional projection, not audited accounting. Rounding to 10% keeps numbers readable without overstating precision. Earlier we rounded to 100%, which collapsed every ROI under ~150% to 0% or 100% — it actively misled partners. 10% preserves meaningful resolution.

8Known limitations

Honest caveats — this is a directional pre-sales tool, not an audited NPV model.

What's in the formula today

Quantified FTE recapture, error reduction, outsourced-spend avoidance.

Asked but not yet in the dollar formula Revenue uplift, risk exposure, compliance cost. Captured in the survey for narrative context; wiring them into the base total is scoped as a follow-up.

Qualitative only Strategic (vendor independence, sovereignty, data gravity). Narrated by the LLM, not dollar-denominated — on purpose.

Known simplifications

Year-one only. No NPV, no multi-year payback. Benefit figures are annualised first-year recurring.

No implementation cost line. The tier cost is recurring (annual SaaS-equivalent). One-time integration / change-management spend isn't modelled — partners quote those separately.

Banded inputs collapse variance. A "<$100K" error-cost answer looks the same as $10K or $90K.

Cycle time is asked but not dollar-scored. Collected to inform the qualitative narrative, not the formula.

Missing inputs default to $0. A respondent who leaves "Cost / FTE" blank silently drops the FTE recapture term from their base total. This surfaces as a "LOW confidence" flag on the use-case card.

How to read the output: treat the base ROI % as a directional prompt for a follow-up conversation, not as a committed number. The range conservative → optimistic fan-out exists specifically so partners can anchor discussions at the low end if a prospect is risk-averse.
